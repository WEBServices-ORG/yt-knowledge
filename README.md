# yt-knowledge

**yt-knowledge** is a research-oriented tool for extracting, processing, and analyzing subtitle content from YouTube channels.

It is designed for **researchers, educators, and independent analysts** who want to work with spoken content at scale â€” without manually downloading, cleaning, or translating subtitles.

This project focuses on **clarity, ethics, and reproducibility**, not growth hacking or content scraping for redistribution.

---

## What does it do?

- Downloads available subtitles from YouTube channels
- Falls back to **local speech-to-text** when subtitles are missing
- Cleans and chunks text intelligently
- Translates content (e.g. to Hebrew)
- Enables search, summarization, and export for research use

You end up with **clean, structured text** ready for analysis.

---

## Who is this for?

- Academic or independent researchers
- NLP / digital humanities projects
- Educational analysis
- Personal research and study

**Not intended for:**
- Re-uploading copyrighted material
- Mass content scraping for commercial redistribution

---

## High-level workflow

1. Select a YouTube channel or video set
2. Extract subtitles (or transcribe audio if needed)
3. Process and normalize the text
4. Translate (optional)
5. Analyze, search, or export results
